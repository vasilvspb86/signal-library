# Data, Decisions & Clinics – The Judgment Layer

> Establish who holds legitimate authority to interpret personal health data when a consumer AI synthesis layer arrives in the consultation room before the clinician does.

## Anti-hype marker
The threat is not diagnosis replacement but premature closure — the invisible interpretive choices embedded in a 'summary' that reads as authoritative

## FROM -> TO
- **From:** Clinical reasoning began with the patient's own symptom account; the physician was the first interpretive layer between raw experience and diagnosis.
- **To:** Consumer AI now pre-synthesises medical records, wearable data, and health history before the clinical encounter begins, making the physician's reasoning contingent on a model output they cannot audit or interrogate.

## Original source
> Google has shipped a judgment layer. The data layer, wearables, Apple Health, medical records, has existed for years. What is new is the interpretive layer that sits above it: the thing that decides what the data means, what the pattern suggests, and what the user should do about it. In clinical medicine, that layer has a name. It is called diagnosis.
