# "The New Priesthood of AI" — AI as Credit-Decision Mediator

> Protect the interval between being judged and bearing the consequences — the space in which a person can understand, contest, and delay a decision about their financial life.

## Anti-hype marker
Frames AI's danger not as dramatic malfunction but as boring workflow — triage and efficiency that ruins people without a visible perpetrator or a clear point of appeal

## FROM -> TO
- **From:** Institutional credit decisions, however imperfect, had identifiable human actors, navigable appeal processes, and a gap between judgment and consequence that borrowers could use
- **To:** AI compresses the gap between classification and consequence, issuing denials through automated chains where accountability blurs and the burden of proof shifts onto the person denied

> AI narrows the time between being interpreted and being acted upon. It compresses the gap between judgment and consequence. By the time a person realizes what has happened, the denial has already gone through, the flag has already been raised, the review has already been routed, the burden has already shifted onto them to prove they are not what the system inferred.
