# EU AI Act Article 9 Building Automation Conformity Deadline

> Protect building occupants' right to control over their own movement, identity, and behavioural data by requiring AI systems that fuse badge, sensor, and calendar signals to meet explicit consent gating and privacy-by-design standards before deployment.

## Anti-hype marker
Privacy broker on every PII fusion is now an Article 9 legal requirement, not a best-practice add-on, for AI systems reading sensor + badge + calendar data

## FROM -> TO
- **From:** Badge, sensor, and reservation data freely fused by AI building-intelligence platforms as anonymised operational telemetry with no consent or audit requirement
- **To:** EU AI Act Article 9 + GDPR interplay requires a privacy broker enforcing differential privacy, k-anonymity floors, and runtime consent audit before any PII fusion is permitted; absence of this architecture is a procurement disqualifier from August 2026

> Privacy broker on every PII fusion (badge × sensor × reservation). The dynamic-occupancy product class — fused signals across access control, IoT, and reservations — is the one Article 9 reviewers and the EDPB are watching most closely. GDPR Article 9, Colorado biometric, SG PDPA, and the EU AI Act all require explicit consent gating + differential privacy / k-anonymity floors before fusion is permitted.
