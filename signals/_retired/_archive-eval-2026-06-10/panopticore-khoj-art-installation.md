# Panopticore — Khoj Studios Art Installation

> Expose the commodification of privacy protection itself — the moment when resistance to surveillance becomes a retail product to be purchased.

## Anti-hype marker
A pop-up retail installation — not a protest, not a report — uses the form of high-fashion commerce to critique commercial surveillance, making the contradiction tangible rather than abstract

> Panopticore is a speculative art project conceived as a pop-up retail installation. It draws inspiration from Jeremy Bentham's Panopticon, Michel Foucault's notion of the disciplinary society, and Shoshana Zuboff's theory of surveillance capitalism, and asks: What happens when privacy is packaged, branded, and sold back to us?
