# "The Death of the Secret" — Radical Inferability Essay

> Protect the unspoken inner life as the foundational condition of selfhood, intimacy, and political dissent — now that inference can reconstruct what was never disclosed.

## Anti-hype marker
The essay argues that the inherited surveillance critique — centred on watching, collecting, and breaching — is structurally inadequate for inference, and that the right we actually need to defend is not privacy but opacity: the right to remain irreducible to any computed portrait.

## FROM -> TO
- **From:** Privacy law and social norms were built around the movement of existing facts — collection, disclosure, breach — all of which presuppose that a sensitive truth pre-exists somewhere and must be transported to cause harm.
- **To:** AI inference manufactures sensitive facts — location, income, sexual orientation, pregnancy — from ambient signals that were individually innocuous, bypassing consent, anonymisation, and every legal protection designed for a world of records rather than synthesis. 'The secret is not pried loose; it is reconstructed from the outside.'
