# Lu Yang – DOKU The Illusion at Venice

> Understand what remains of personal identity when a digital avatar can reincarnate, multiply, and persist beyond the physical self that created it.

## Anti-hype marker
Lu Yang frames digital reincarnation not as liberation but as a new form of attachment — if consciousness relocates to a digital body, the prison has only changed its form, not disappeared.

## FROM -> TO
- **From:** Identity was understood as singular, embodied, and bounded by a biological life; the self and its representations were clearly distinguishable.
- **To:** Digital avatars derived from a living person's body can develop autonomous presence, move between virtual worlds, and persist in ways that dissolve the boundary between creator and created — the work makes that instability physically inhabitable.
