> Amazon agreed to a $309.5 million class action settlement to resolve allegations that it routinely failed to refund customers who returned products and, in many cases, reversed instant refunds and re-charged their accounts weeks later. The settlement, filed January 23, 2026, is one of the largest e-commerce consumer refund settlements in U.S. history. When combined with the roughly $570 million Amazon had already issued in direct refunds and $363 million in non-monetary relief to overhaul its return infrastructure, the total settlement value exceeds $1 billion.
