# Expedia Group / Split-Funnel Distribution Research

> Protect travelers' boundaries by preserving their control over high-stakes booking decisions, refusing to delegate payment and reservation commitment to an AI agent they do not yet trust.

## Anti-hype marker
Structural trust barrier — not a temporary adoption lag — is splitting the AI-assisted funnel and preventing agentic booking conversion.

## FROM -> TO
- **From:** AI agents predicted to own the full hospitality booking funnel — discovery through transaction — replacing OTAs and direct channels as the single conversion point.
- **To:** Travelers actively bifurcate the funnel: using AI for discovery while deliberately switching to trusted human-web channels to complete the transaction, driven by payment security, data privacy, and loss-of-control concerns.

## Original source
> The specific concerns Expedia surfaced: payment security, data privacy, loss of control, and the absence of clear customer support if something goes wrong. Agentic booking requires the traveler to hand a credit card, a set of preferences, and a reservation commitment to a chat interface. Most travelers will not do that.
