# Freddie Yauner — "What I'm Looking At" Mirror Mask

> Shift attention from the surveilled face back toward what the person sees — reclaiming the act of looking as outward orientation rather than inward identity capture.

## Anti-hype marker
The work achieves mass cultural reach (10M+ views, 1M+ likes) precisely because it is analogue, non-technological, and anti-product — a handmade object that names the surveillance condition more effectively than any privacy app.

## FROM -> TO
- **From:** The face in public space is the default unit of identity, recognition, and surveillance — a surface read by others rather than a window the wearer controls.
- **To:** A viral wearable art project replaces the face with a mirror of the surrounding world, reaching 10 million views as a cultural gesture that names — before any product does — the desire to opt out of facial legibility in public space.
