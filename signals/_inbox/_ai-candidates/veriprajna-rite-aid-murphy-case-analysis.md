# Rite Aid FTC Ban & Harvey Murphy False Arrest — Enterprise AI Failure Analysis

> Verify that an automated identification system is actually trustworthy before it is empowered to take actions that remove a person's freedom.

## Anti-hype marker
The Rite Aid systems had 98% claimed accuracy — the failure was not the number but the absence of uncertainty quantification, open-set design, and genuine human review, exposing the gap between vendor marketing and production safety.

## FROM -> TO
- **From:** Retailers and their vendors treated facial recognition match scores as authoritative verdicts, deploying systems without accuracy testing, demographic bias evaluation, or meaningful human review — because no regulation required them to.
- **To:** The FTC banned Rite Aid from facial recognition for five years and ordered model disgorgement; a $10M lawsuit followed a wrongful arrest caused by a false match — establishing that 'the AI did it' is not an acceptable liability defence and that architecture choices carry real legal consequences.
