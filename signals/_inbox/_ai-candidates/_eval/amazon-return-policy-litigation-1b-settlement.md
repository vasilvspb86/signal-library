## Original source
> Judge Whitehead's language in the motion-to-dismiss ruling is worth noting. He did not describe the alleged conduct as a contractual dispute about return policy terms. He described it as Amazon having "stolen money directly from their bank accounts." That framing reflects the seriousness with which the court viewed the core allegation: that Amazon was not just failing to issue refunds, it was actively taking money back from customers who had no idea it was gone.
