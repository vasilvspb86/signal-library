# Max Bleyleben — 'How Not To Catch a Shoplifter'

> Protect against automated decision systems that assign consequences without the human subject having any ability to see, understand, or contest the machine's output.

## Anti-hype marker
The FTC's enforcement lever is Section 5 of the FTC Act — a 1914 statute repurposed for AI regulation — meaning accountability for algorithmic harm already exists in US law without any new legislation.

## FROM -> TO
- **From:** Consumer protection enforcement in the US addressed visible, human-initiated unfair practices; automated decision-making by deployed AI systems fell outside a clear regulatory framework.
- **To:** The FTC's Rite Aid action established that deploying AI systems causing consumer harm through bias, inaccuracy, or opacity constitutes an unfair trade practice under existing law — creating a de-facto regulatory floor for retail AI before Congress has legislated.
