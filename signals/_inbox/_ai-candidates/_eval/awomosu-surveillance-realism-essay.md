# Abi Awomosu — 'They Built a System to See Everything'

> Reclaim a sense of agency over your own attention, desires, and movement in a world where the infrastructure of commercial and digital space has been designed to convert your presence into a continuously monitored asset.

## Anti-hype marker
The essay names 'surveillance realism' — a learned cultural helplessness that normalises total monitoring — as more corrosive than any single technology, because it forecloses the imagination of alternatives.

## FROM -> TO
- **From:** Surveillance and data collection were experienced as discrete, noticeable events that required active participation (loyalty cards, cookies, app downloads).
- **To:** Extraction has become ambient: the ceiling of a supermarket, the phone in your pocket, and the face-scanning camera at the door all operate as a seamless, invisible apparatus that converts physical presence into a data surface without any deliberate act of consent.

## Original source
> Surveillance realism — the simultaneous unease with data collection alongside the active normalisation of surveillance that limits the possibility of imagining things being different. Privacy cynicism— an attitude of uncertainty, powerlessness, and mistrust that renders privacy protection subjectively futile. Data disaffection — not apathy, something more precise. An intentional numbness adopted to sustain yourself in the face of something that feels total and inevitable.
