# AI.Mirror — 185 Years of Commercial Surveillance (Substack)

> Preserve the boundary between what a person chooses to disclose and what institutions extract from them without consent — a boundary being erased layer by layer across two centuries of commercial data practice.

## Anti-hype marker
The essay frames AI prompt data not as a new category of risk but as the culmination of a 185-year commercial surveillance architecture — repositioning the current debate from 'AI privacy' to structural continuity that makes the present moment legible as inevitable rather than surprising.

## FROM -> TO
- **From:** Surveillance was understood as discrete acts of collection — a file opened, a record stored, a camera aimed — each individually contestable and regulatable.
- **To:** AI assistants now receive unguarded reasoning — 'the prompt is what you were going to search for after you finished thinking it through, with the anxiety still visible in the sentence structure' — making the gap between thought and data extraction smaller than any prior surveillance technology achieved, at a scale of 800 million weekly users.
