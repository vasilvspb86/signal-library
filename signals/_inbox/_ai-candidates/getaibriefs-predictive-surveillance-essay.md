# "Predictive AI: When Surveillance Stops Watching and Starts Deciding"

> Demand that automated systems which determine access, freedom, and suspicion remain contestable by the humans they judge — before prediction replaces evidence as the basis for consequential decisions.

## Anti-hype marker
The retail shoplifting camera is used as the concrete entry point to illustrate predictive surveillance in its most quotidian, socially accepted form — making visible a logic that most people encounter daily without recognising its structural implications.

## FROM -> TO
- **From:** Surveillance documented what had happened, producing evidence a person could contest. Human oversight was slow and expensive but structurally present.
- **To:** AI surveillance predicts who will act before they act, producing probabilistic verdicts from training data that encodes historical bias — and the person subject to the verdict typically has no right to know an algorithm was involved, let alone to challenge it.
