# Bjørn Broum / Data Platform as Governance Principle

> Establish control over operational decisions by making data trustworthy at the point where it originates, rather than by moving it to a place where it can be controlled.

## Anti-hype marker
The argument is that centralisation is governance risk in disguise — moving data strips its lineage and operational context, making the reconstructed version less trustworthy than the original.

## FROM -> TO
- **From:** Data governance meant centralizing all data into a warehouse or lake before it could be trusted — a model that introduced latency, broke real-time context, and caused data to lose the operational state it held at creation.
- **To:** The platform is redefined as a consistent set of contracts, controls, and semantics enforced wherever data lives — enabling AI agents operating at the point of action to reason on current, contextually intact data without waiting for a pipeline.
